Major Sensors on Oceanography Satellites
Sensor Main Purpose Technology Type Typical Frequency / Band Example Missions/Satellites
Radar Altimeter Measures sea surface height for sea level, currents, and waves (and elevation/freeboard over ice). Active radar (nadir; pulse-limited or SAR/Delay-Doppler) Typically Ku 13.575 GHz + C ~5.3–5.41 GHz; some systems also use Ka ~35.75 GHz TOPEX/Poseidon, Jason-3, Sentinel-6, Sentinel-3A/B (SRAL), CryoSat-2 (SIRAL), HY-2B
Scatterometer Measures near-surface ocean wind vectors from radar backscatter over the sea surface. Active radar (real-aperture or pencil-beam scanning) C-band ~5.255 GHz or Ku-band ~13.4–14.6 GHz MetOp-A/B/C (ASCAT), QuikSCAT (SeaWinds), Oceansat-2 (OSCAT), HY-2B (SCAT), CFOSAT (SCAT), Seasat (SASS)
Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Images ocean roughness patterns to map winds, waves, fronts, sea ice, and ships in all weather. Active radar imaging (SAR; can support InSAR for phase-based products) Commonly C 5.405 GHz; also L ~1.27 GHz and X ~9.65 GHz Sentinel-1A/B, RADARSAT-2, TerraSAR-X/TanDEM-X, ALOS-2 (PALSAR-2), Seasat (SAR)
Microwave Radiometer (Passive) Corrects radar altimetry for atmospheric water vapor and cloud liquid water (wet-tropospheric delay). Passive microwave radiometer Often ~18.7, 23.8, 34–36.5 GHz (K/Ka window + water vapor channels) Jason-3 (AMR), Sentinel-3 (MWR), Sentinel-6 (AMR-C), SWOT (AMR), HY-2 (MWR)
Ocean Color Radiometer Measures water-leaving radiance to estimate chlorophyll, turbidity, and other ocean color products. Passive optical (multispectral / imaging spectrometer) Visible–NIR ~0.4–1.0 μm (e.g., ~400–1020 nm) Sentinel-3 (OLCI), Aqua (MODIS), Suomi NPP/NOAA-20 (VIIRS), SeaWiFS (OrbView-2), Envisat (MERIS), Nimbus-7 (CZCS)
Infrared Radiometer (SST) Measures sea surface temperature using thermal infrared “split-window” channels (best in clear skies). Passive thermal infrared radiometer Thermal IR ~3.7 μm and ~10.8–12 μm (common SST channels) Sentinel-3 (SLSTR), Aqua (MODIS), Suomi NPP/NOAA-20 (VIIRS), NOAA series (AVHRR), Envisat (AATSR), ERS-2 (ATSR-2)
L-band Radiometer (Salinity) Measures sea surface salinity from L-band brightness temperature (with corrections for roughness and temperature). Passive microwave radiometer (real aperture or interferometric) L-band 1.413 GHz (≈21 cm) SMOS (MIRAS), Aquarius/SAC-D (L-band radiometers), SMAP (L-band radiometer)
DORIS Provides precise orbit determination by measuring Doppler shift from a global network of ground beacons. Doppler radio navigation (dual-frequency) 2.03625 GHz (S-band) + 401.25 MHz (UHF) for ionospheric correction TOPEX/Poseidon, Jason-3, Sentinel-3A/B, Sentinel-6, CryoSat-2, SARAL/AltiKa
GNSS Receiver (POD) Tracks GPS/GNSS signals to support precise orbit determination (critical for accurate sea level products). Passive GNSS receiver Commonly GPS L1 1575.42 MHz and L2 1227.60 MHz (and/or other GNSS signals) Sentinel-3A/B (GPS), Sentinel-6 (GNSS), SWOT (GPSP), Jason-3 (GPS), HY-2A/2B (GPS), Sentinel-1 (GPS)
Laser Retroreflector Array (LRA) Enables ground-based Satellite Laser Ranging to validate or refine precise orbits. Optical retroreflector (used by ground laser systems) Optical (commonly 532 nm and/or 1064 nm lasers from the ground) TOPEX/Poseidon, Jason-1/2/3, Sentinel-3A/B, Sentinel-6, SWOT, CryoSat-2

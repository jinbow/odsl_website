Major Sensors on Oceanography Satellites
Sensor Main Purpose Technology Type Typical Frequency / Band Example Missions/Satellites
Radar Altimeter (Nadir) Measures sea surface height (1D profile), wave height, and wind speed. Active radar (nadir; pulse-limited, SAR, or LRM) Standard: Ku ~13.6 GHz + C ~5.3 GHz (Jason, Sentinel-6, SWOT Poseidon-3C).
Exceptions: Envisat (Ku + S-band ~3.2 GHz); SARAL (Ka-band ~35.75 GHz only). 		Jason-3, Sentinel-6, Sentinel-3 (SRAL), SWOT (Poseidon-3C), Envisat (RA-2), SARAL (AltiKa)
Radar Interferometer (KaRIn) Measures 2D sea surface height images (wide-swath ~120km) to resolve mesoscale eddies & river levels. Wide-swath Interferometric SAR (InSAR) Ka-band (35.75 GHz) with two antennas on a 10m boom SWOT (KaRIn)
Scatterometer Measures near-surface ocean wind vectors from radar backscatter. Active radar (fan-beam or rotating pencil-beam) C-band: ~5.255 GHz (MetOp ASCAT).
Ku-band: ~13.2–13.5 GHz (QuikSCAT, HY-2, CFOSAT SCAT).
L-band: 1.26 GHz (Aquarius - primarily for roughness correction). 		MetOp (ASCAT), QuikSCAT, HY-2B/C/D, CFOSAT (SCAT), Aquarius
Wave Spectrometer (SWIM) Measures directional wave spectra (wavelength, direction) using rotating beams at multiple incidence angles. Real-aperture radar (6 rotating beams) Ku-band (13.575 GHz) CFOSAT (SWIM)
Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Images ocean roughness patterns to map winds, waves, fronts, sea ice, and ships in all weather. Active radar imaging Commonly C 5.405 GHz (Sentinel-1); also L ~1.27 GHz (ALOS) and X ~9.65 GHz (TerraSAR-X) Sentinel-1A/B/C, RADARSAT, ALOS-2, TerraSAR-X
Microwave Radiometer (Passive) Corrects radar altimetry for atmospheric water vapor (wet-troposphere delay). Passive microwave radiometer Classic: 18.7, 23.8, 36.5 GHz.
New (Sentinel-6): Includes 34.0 GHz (AMR-C) for coastal stability.
SWOT/Jason: 18.7, 23.8, 34.0 GHz. 		Jason-3 (AMR), Sentinel-3 (MWR), Sentinel-6 (AMR-C), SWOT (AMR), Envisat (MWR)
Ocean Color Radiometer Measures water-leaving radiance to estimate chlorophyll, turbidity, and CDOM. Passive optical (multispectral / imaging spectrometer) Visible–NIR ~400 nm to ~1020 nm (Sentinel-3 OLCI) Sentinel-3 (OLCI), Aqua (MODIS), VIIRS (Suomi-NPP/NOAA-20), Envisat (MERIS)
Infrared Radiometer (SST) Measures sea surface temperature using thermal infrared “split-window” channels. Passive thermal infrared radiometer Thermal IR ~3.7 μm and ~10.8–12 μm Sentinel-3 (SLSTR), Aqua (MODIS), VIIRS, AVHRR, Envisat (AATSR)
L-band Radiometer (Salinity) Measures sea surface salinity from L-band brightness temperature. Passive microwave radiometer L-band 1.413 GHz (≈21 cm) SMOS (MIRAS), Aquarius, SMAP
DORIS Precise orbit determination via Doppler shift from ground beacons. Doppler radio navigation 2.036 GHz (S-band) + 401.25 MHz (UHF) Jason-3, Sentinel-3, Sentinel-6, CryoSat-2, SARAL/AltiKa, Envisat
GNSS Receiver (POD) Tracks GPS/GNSS signals for precise orbit determination. Passive GNSS receiver GPS L1/L2, Galileo, etc. Sentinel-1, Sentinel-3, Sentinel-6, SWOT, Jason-3
Laser Retroreflector Array (LRA) Ground-based Satellite Laser Ranging for orbit validation. Optical retroreflector Optical (Green 532 nm / IR 1064 nm) Most Altimetry Missions (Jason, Sentinel-3, Sentinel-6, SWOT)

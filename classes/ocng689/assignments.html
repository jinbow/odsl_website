Assignments

This page lists all major deliverables for OCNG 689. Homework is designed to build practical competency with satellite oceanography data products and interpretation. Graduate students additionally complete literature reviews and a term project.

Submission

  • Submit through Canvas unless otherwise specified.
  • Include figures with labeled axes, units, and short captions.
  • Code must be reproducible (scripts/notebooks + brief README of steps).

Default Due Pattern

  • Assignments are typically released after Tuesday lecture.
  • Due the following Tuesday (unless a different date is stated).
  • Spring Break week has no due items.

Academic Integrity

  • You may discuss concepts and approaches with classmates.
  • Your submitted work (writing, code, figures) must be your own.
  • Cite any external sources, including code snippets and AI assistance.

Instructor note: The item list below mirrors the schedule page. If you revise dates in schedule.html, update them here as well to keep a single source of truth.

Homework (All Students)

Problem sets and applied mini-analyses
HW 1 — EM wave calculations Due Jan 27

Core EM relationships, radiometric quantities, unit handling, and basic atmospheric transmission reasoning. Aligned with Module 2.

HW 2 — Sensor types and functionalities Available Jan 27 • Due Feb 3

Identify passive vs active sensors, typical bands, and which ocean variables each supports; interpret Level 1–4 product concepts. Aligned with Module 3.

HW 3 — Chlorophyll analysis Due Feb 10

Simple ocean color analysis workflow (masking, compositing, and interpretation of spatial patterns). Aligned with Module 4.

HW 4 — SST case study Due Feb 17

SST retrieval interpretation and an oceanographic feature case (fronts, eddies, or upwelling). Aligned with Module 5.

HW 5 — Wind/SST analysis Due Feb 24

Compare wind and SST patterns; discuss plausible coupling mechanisms and observational limitations. Aligned with Module 6.

HW 6 — Scatterometry application Due Mar 3

Analyze wind vectors from scatterometry and connect signatures to weather/ocean context. Aligned with Module 7.

HW 7 — Altimetry analysis Due Mar 17

Interpret SSH/SSHA products, apply basic corrections reasoning, and infer geostrophic flow patterns. Aligned with Module 8.

HW 8 — Coastal SAR case Due (week of Mar 16–20)

SAR-based coastal interpretation: surface roughness signatures, slicks, or wave/current interactions. Aligned with Module 9.

HW 9 — Gravity/Lidar case Due Mar 31

Interpret GRACE/ICESat-2 type products conceptually; connect measurement physics to observables and uncertainty. Aligned with Module 10.

HW 10 — Ocean dynamics from space Due Apr 7

Use satellite products to reason about circulation/eddies; emphasize interpretation over algorithmic detail. Aligned with Module 11.

HW 11 — ENSO/Sea level / Sea ice case Due Apr 14

Large-scale application analysis using multi-sensor products; focus on signal vs. artifacts and uncertainty. Aligned with Module 12.

Graduate Deliverables

Literature reviews + term project milestones

Literature Reviews (4)

Each review should provide: (1) context and research question, (2) methods and data, (3) key results, (4) limitations/uncertainty, (5) implications for satellite oceanography, and (6) your critique and proposed next steps.

  • Lit Review 1: Ocean color — Due Feb 26
  • Lit Review 2: SST/Wind — Due Mar 19
  • Lit Review 3: SAR — Due Apr 2
  • Lit Review 4: Eddy — Due Apr 16
Open literature page

Term Project

A focused research-style analysis using one or more satellite datasets. The project should include a clear objective, data description, method, results, and uncertainty-aware interpretation.

  • Topic/Proposal: Due Mar 26
  • Draft: Due Apr 23
See grading & format

Midterm Exam

In-class exam covering Weeks 1–7 (fundamentals through scatterometry), emphasizing conceptual mastery and defensible reasoning with equations and units.

  • Date: Mar 5
